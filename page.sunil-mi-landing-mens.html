What you should know for the best results

The Qure Micro-Infusion System is designed with your safety in mind.

Unlike popular at-home derma rollers, our stamper device features single-use sterile heads that help minimize the risk of infection or unnecessary skin damage caused by blunt and multi-use needles.

Below are a few rules you should follow while using your Qure Micro-Infusion System:

How To Use

Fill the device: Open the device and pour in one ampoule of serum.
Prepare the device: Twist on the needle head and leave upside down for 1–2 minutes.
Begin infusion: Stamp evenly across the face with 2–3 passes, then pat in any remaining serum.
Tip: Apply remaining serum after your treatment and allow it to absorb overnight.

Safety Guidelines

Micro-Infusion is a simple treatment you can do at-home. No appointments and no downtime. It’s designed to be safe and easy to use, as long as your skin is clean and healthy.
Avoid using Micro-Infusion on active breakouts or broken skin, as it may spread bacteria. Always use a fresh, sterile needle head for each treatment, and apply SPF after use to protect your skin.
If you’re pregnant or have a medical condition, check with your doctor first.

If you live in Australia, you may receive a notice from Border Force regarding tranexamic acid (TXA) in your order. Here’s what you need to know:

In Australia, TXA is classified as a Schedule 4 (prescription-only) drug only when used orally or by injection for treating melasma or other medical conditions. However, topical TXA in cosmetic formulations is permitted.

Our serums are purely for cosmetic use and do not claim to treat medical conditions. They are applied topically (not injected) and are classified as a cosmetic product rather than a prescription medicine.

Our products are compliant with Australian regulartions. Facial serums containing up to 3% TXA for cosmetic purposes are not classified as prescription-only medicines and do not require inclusion in the Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods (ARTG).
